# Competency Framework

The competency framework defines what the Finance Director Scenario Coach should help learners practice and improve as the first FinanceOS product. It should remain small enough to be usable in the MVP, while leaving room for future expansion.

## Core Competencies

### 1. Financial Insight

Ability to interpret financial information and identify the business meaning behind the numbers.

Observable behaviors:

- Separates signal from noise.
- Connects financial movements to operating drivers.
- Identifies missing information and key assumptions.
- Avoids overclaiming when data is incomplete.

### 2. Commercial Judgment

Ability to evaluate tradeoffs between growth, margin, cash, risk, and execution capacity.

Observable behaviors:

- Frames options with business consequences.
- Considers timing and opportunity cost.
- Identifies second-order effects.
- Makes recommendations rather than only reporting facts.

### 3. Cash And Risk Discipline

Ability to protect liquidity, controls, and resilience without blocking useful action.

Observable behaviors:

- Recognizes cash constraints early.
- Balances runway, working capital, and investment needs.
- Escalates material risks clearly.
- Distinguishes manageable risk from unacceptable exposure.

### 4. Stakeholder Communication

Ability to communicate finance perspectives clearly to non-finance stakeholders.

Observable behaviors:

- Uses concise executive language.
- Adapts message to audience.
- Makes tradeoffs understandable.
- Challenges constructively.

### 5. Strategic Leadership

Ability to operate as a business leader, not only a finance expert.

Observable behaviors:

- Aligns finance recommendations with company strategy.
- Shows ownership of outcomes.
- Helps leadership teams make decisions.
- Anticipates board, investor, lender, or auditor concerns.

## Scoring Guidance

Initial scoring can be qualitative:

- `Developing`: Response misses important context, risks, or tradeoffs.
- `Capable`: Response handles the core issue with reasonable judgment.
- `Strong`: Response shows clear finance leadership, prioritization, and communication.

Numerical scoring can be added later if it improves feedback quality. It should not replace clear written coaching.

## Framework Boundaries

This framework is not a full finance curriculum. It exists to guide scenario feedback and competency tracking. Technical accounting, tax, treasury, and corporate finance topics may appear inside scenarios, but they should be evaluated through practical leadership behavior.
