# Learning Domain Framework

This framework defines the finance-domain knowledge FinanceOS should prioritize. It is separate from the behavioral competency framework in `docs/learning/competency-framework.md`.

The behavioral competency framework describes how a learner thinks, communicates, and leads. This domain framework describes what finance topic the learner is practicing.

## Priority Order

### 1. Balance Sheet

Balance Sheet understanding is the first priority because Finance Directors need to see how operating decisions affect assets, liabilities, equity, resilience, and hidden risk.

Initial learning focus:

- Working capital movement.
- Receivables, payables, inventory, and accruals.
- Capitalized versus expensed decisions.
- Debt, provisions, and contingent liabilities.
- Balance sheet quality and risk indicators.

### 2. Cash Flow

Cash Flow is the second priority because strong reported performance can still create liquidity pressure. Learners should understand why profit and cash diverge.

Initial learning focus:

- Operating cash flow.
- EBITDA to cash conversion.
- Runway and burn rate.
- Hiring, growth, and cash timing.
- Cash implications of working capital and capex.

### 3. Treasury

Treasury is the third priority because Finance Directors must manage liquidity, funding, banking relationships, and financial risk.

Initial learning focus:

- Liquidity planning.
- Funding options and constraints.
- Covenant awareness.
- Foreign exchange and interest exposure where relevant.
- Cash controls and approval discipline.

### 4. Audit

Audit is the fourth priority because Finance Directors must protect reporting quality, control maturity, and stakeholder trust.

Initial learning focus:

- Audit readiness.
- Control weaknesses.
- Revenue recognition and cut-off awareness.
- Management judgment and evidence quality.
- Auditor, board, and management communication.

### 5. Broader Finance Director Topics

Broader Finance Director topics should be introduced after the core financial foundations are represented in scenarios.

Initial learning focus:

- Forecasting and planning.
- Board reporting.
- Pricing and margin management.
- Investment cases.
- Business partnering.
- Team leadership.
- Governance and decision cadence.

## Usage In Scenarios

Each scenario should identify one primary domain and optional secondary domains. Domain tags should guide scenario selection and debrief content, while behavioral competency ratings should guide feedback on judgment and communication.

## MVP Priority

The first scenario combines Balance Sheet and Cash Flow, with Treasury implications. Its approved specification is [Scenario 001: Growth With Falling Cash](scenarios/scenario-001-growth-with-falling-cash.md).
