# Product Principles

## 1. Practice Before Content

The product should emphasize active decision practice over passive reading. Explanations are useful when they help the learner improve a decision, not as the primary experience.

## 2. Finance Leadership, Not Generic Coaching

Feedback should be grounded in Finance Director and CFO realities: cash, controls, forecasting, commercial tradeoffs, investor confidence, board communication, risk, and operating discipline.

## 3. Simple First, Expand Deliberately

The first product should prove the scenario coaching loop. Add persistence, AI roleplay, rich financial packs, and dashboards only when their use case is clear.

## 4. Realistic Ambiguity

Scenarios should avoid tidy textbook answers. Senior finance work often involves incomplete data, stakeholder tension, timing pressure, and competing good options.

## 5. Transparent Evaluation

Learners should understand why feedback was given. Scoring should connect to visible competencies, observable behaviors, and scenario-specific expectations.

## 6. Respect The Learner

The tone should be direct, useful, and professional. The coach should stretch the learner without being theatrical, patronizing, or artificially harsh.

## 7. Modular Learning

Scenarios, competencies, scoring rubrics, financial packs, and learner progress should be separable concepts so the platform can evolve without rewriting the product.

## 8. Documentation Is Product Infrastructure

Because this is an early-stage product, written decisions are part of the system. Important assumptions and tradeoffs should be captured as the product changes.
